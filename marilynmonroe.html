Marilyn Monroe became the dazzling icon of the the 40s and 50s, bringing her beauty and talent into the movie industry. However, not everything was all praise for Monroe. She was one of the first women to express herself as an activist, for both gender equality and sexual assault. In the biography Goddess: The Secret Life of Marilyn Monroe, the actress revealed that she "spent a great deal of time on my knees" over her career. She was repeatedly silenced by men in positions of power, and later she said, "Most of the men are such horrors. they deserve all they can get out of them!"

Famous and legendary actresses managed to share their stories of abuse and mistreatment as well. Shirley Temple revealed that in 1940, when she was 12, an MGM producer exposed his body parts to her during their meeting. Dame Joan Collins tested for the leading role of Cleopatra in the 1963 film, but lost it when she refused to sleep with the studio boss.Dame Helen Mirren was sexualized during an interview with TV director Michael Winner.
